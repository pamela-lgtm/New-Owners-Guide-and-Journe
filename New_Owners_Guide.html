Stage 1

We've decided we're a good fit. Now what?

We know this is a big step — handing over the keys to something you've invested in takes real trust. We don't take that lightly. Getting started with Verandah is designed to feel easy, not overwhelming, from the very first moment.

SetupWhat is the $500 owner reserve and why is it required?+

Before we market your property, we require a $500 operating reserve held in our trust account. This is not a fee — it's your money, set aside specifically for your property.

It exists so that when a routine repair comes up — a broken garbage disposal, a clogged drain, a smoke detector — we can handle it immediately without a wire transfer delay from you. Timing matters in property management, and your tenant's experience matters.

If the reserve is used, it's replenished from the next month's rent before your disbursement is sent. You'll see every penny accounted for on your monthly statement.

PrepWhat does "show-ready" actually mean?+

Show-ready means a potential renter can look at your property and genuinely picture moving in that day. It's not about perfection — it's about communicating care and standards.

Interior: Deep clean throughout, fresh neutral paint, all appliances clean and functioning, updated light and plumbing fixtures (within the last 5 years), carpet professionally cleaned or replaced, hard floors clean with no excessive wear.

Exterior: Lawn mowed, trimmed, and edged; irrigation on automatic; fresh mulch in beds; no weeds; power-washed if needed (in Florida, this is typically every year or two); pool clean and blue.

We provide you with our full "Rent Ready" checklist during onboarding — and we're here to guide you through it. This is not meant to overwhelm; it's meant to protect your investment from day one.

You can rent a property that's not quite move-in ready. But we can assure you it won't be to a highly qualified tenant. The quality of what you present communicates what you expect in return.

PrepDo I have to find my own contractors to get it ready?+

Not at all — and we wouldn't want you to feel like you have to figure that out on your own. These are people we've worked with for years and genuinely trust with the properties we manage.

There's something else we love about this part: meeting our vendors during the make-ready process gives you an early, real look at our extended team. By the time your property goes live, you won't just be trusting Verandah — you'll know some of the people caring for your property alongside us. That kind of transparency matters to us, and we think it'll matter to you too.

InsuranceWhat happens to my homeowner's insurance when I rent?+

This is one of the most important things to handle before your property goes live. Your standard homeowner's policy typically does not cover a rental situation — you'll need to convert it to a landlord policy (sometimes called a dwelling fire policy).

You'll also be required to add Verandah Properties as an additional insured on your policy. Minimum coverage required: $100,000 per person / $300,000 per occurrence in public liability.

Here's some good news: during your first year, our Landlord Shield coverage (more on that below) fulfills the additional insured requirement — so you have a grace period to get your policy properly updated without being out of compliance from day one.

We also mandate that your tenants carry a minimum of $100,000 in liability insurance covering damage to the property such as fire or flooding. We strongly encourage them to carry renters insurance for their personal belongings as well, though that protects them — not the property.

Stage 3

Marketing your property the right way.

Once your property is ready, we take over completely. You don't need to manage showings, field calls, or wonder if anyone's looking. We handle all of it — and we keep you informed every step of the way so you always know where things stand.

MarketingWhy don't you list the property until it's fully ready?+

This is a deliberate philosophy — and one that protects you. Today's renters are doing their research entirely online before ever setting foot in a property. The moment a listing goes live, the clock starts ticking on Zillow.

A property that sits on Zillow for 30+ days sends a signal: something's wrong. Even if the reason is simply that the property wasn't ready at listing, the damage to first impressions is real. Highly qualified renters move quickly and pass on anything that looks like it's been sitting.

We also do not show occupied properties. A well-qualified renter wants to see exactly what they're moving into — not someone else's furniture, not a home in mid-transition. We show properties that are vacant, clean, and ready. That's what attracts the tenant you want.

We start the Zillow marketing clock when the property is ready — not before. This is one of the most important decisions in the leasing process and it's one most owners don't think about until it's too late.

MarketingWhere exactly is my property marketed?+

Across all of the channels that matter — and a few that most property managers can't offer:

  • MLS (Multiple Listing Service): Verandah is a licensed Realtor and an active member of the MLS — which means your property is exposed to every Realtor and agent in the area. If they bring a qualified tenant who signs a lease, we pay them from our management fees — nothing additional from you.
  • Zillow with virtual tour and floor plan: Our professional photographer produces a full virtual walkthrough and floor plan — features Zillow actively promotes, putting your listing ahead of the competition.
  • Our website: Verandah specializes in Lake Nona and the Greater Orlando area — your property is featured within our market identity.
  • All major rental platforms: Realtor.com, Apartments.com, Rent.com, and all other high-traffic rental sites.
  • Social media: Instagram and Facebook — properties promoted to our audience and beyond.
ShowingsHow are showings handled — do I need to be available?+

Not at all — and this is actually what modern renters want. Today's qualified prospects expect to tour on their own schedule, not wait for an appointment. We make that possible.

Once your property is show-ready, we install a Rently Smart Lockbox on the property. Qualified prospects can tour at any time that works for them, with no scheduling required from you.

Rently is far more than a lockbox. Before anyone can access it, they must verify their identity through a rigorous process — credit card verification, government ID, and a selfie taken at the property that must match their ID on file. We receive an instant notification every time a showing occurs. In years of using this system, we have not had issues.

All inquiries during business hours are handled by our live team. After hours, your New Client Liaison handles incoming questions to make sure no qualified prospect goes unanswered.

ScreeningHow do you screen applicants?+

Every applicant is evaluated across multiple layers — and every layer has to hold up. We are not a pass/fail checklist. We're looking at the full picture of who this person is.

  • Credit and financial history: The most reliable indicator. We review payment history, outstanding obligations, and overall credit profile — not just a score. We also look at mortgage history, which shows up on the credit report and tells us how someone handles their most significant financial commitment.
  • Income verification: Gross monthly income of at least 2.5–3x the monthly rent, verified through pay stubs, tax returns, or bank statements. We verify the documents — we don't just take them at face value.
  • Rental history: We contact previous landlords directly. How they maintained the property, whether they paid on time, how they communicated, how they left it.
  • Document authenticity: We are trained to identify institutional fraud — including fabricated pay stubs, altered bank statements, and forged ID documents, which have become increasingly sophisticated. Fraudulent applications are caught and rejected.
  • Social media and public record research: We've learned over 20+ years that a quick look at how someone presents themselves publicly adds meaningful context. We use every reasonable tool available to us to understand who we're placing in your home.
  • Application behavior: How quickly someone responds, how they communicate, whether they ask thoughtful questions. These signals have flagged problems more times than we can count — and we pay attention to them.

We continue to add to our screening process as the landscape evolves. Only applicants who meet every standard we set are approved. Every single time.

ScreeningWhat's the deal with Experian reporting — and why does it matter?+

Every month, we report your tenant's payment directly to Experian — on time or late. Our residents know this from day one.

This is a powerful lever. For tenants who care about their credit (and our screening ensures they do), on-time rent payment is a direct investment in their financial profile. It keeps accountability front and center throughout the tenancy — not just at move-in.

It also means our well-qualified residents have real motivation beyond just avoiding late fees. Their credit score is on the line every single month. That changes behavior in the best possible way.

ScreeningWhat about pets?+

Our listings note that "pets will be considered." This is a deliberate policy — refusing all pets eliminates more than 50% of qualified renters immediately. The well-qualified tenants we work to place are responsible people who will not allow an animal to damage a home they value.

Every pet applicant goes through our professional pet screening service — it's part of the formal application process. Aggressive breeds are not permitted. Approved pets are documented; any unauthorized additions are a lease violation.

ESAs and service animals are processed through a verifiable application to ensure legal compliance.

Additionally, your property is enrolled in our Animal Protection Guarantee — up to $1,500 in pet-related damage coverage beyond the security deposit, included at no charge.

HOAWhat happens if my HOA sends a violation?+

We handle all HOA violations — whether they're tenant-caused or owner-responsibility — from start to close. Fully documented, every time.

For tenant-caused violations, we use lease-based leverage to manage and deter repeat behavior. HOAs have the ability to impose fines, and we take compliance seriously. Our process ensures violations are addressed promptly so they don't escalate.

All HOA fees and assessments remain the owner's financial responsibility. We monitor, manage, and close out violations — you cover the underlying HOA obligations.

FinanceWhen do I get paid — and what's deducted first?+

Our accounting is overseen by a CPA — and owner disbursements go out on the 8th of every month (or the next business day if the 8th falls on a weekend when our administrative office is closed). By the 8th, on-time tenant payments have cleared and your proceeds are ready.

Disbursements happen in this order: our management fees → any repair or vendor invoices → reserve replenishment → remaining balance to you. Everything is itemized on your monthly statement. Nothing is hidden, nothing is rounded up.

One more thing worth knowing: we maintain after-hours emergency maintenance dispatch for your residents. If something urgent happens at 10pm, your tenant has a way to reach us. Your investment and your tenant are covered around the clock.

ContactWhy can't I contact my tenant directly?+

We know this can feel counterintuitive — it's your property, after all. But this boundary is one of the most important ways we protect you, and once you understand why, it makes complete sense.

If you contact your tenant directly and a dispute later arises — about anything — those communications can become evidence in a legal proceeding. The moment you're in the chain of communication, you're also in the chain of liability. That's a risk we want to keep entirely off your plate.

Practically speaking, direct owner contact also makes it harder for us to enforce the lease on your behalf. If a tenant knows they can reach you directly, our ability to hold them accountable becomes much more difficult — and that ultimately hurts you.

Think of it the way you'd lean on an attorney to handle communication in a legal matter rather than calling the other party yourself. It's not distrust — it's smart protection. You have a whole team to handle this. Let us do our job for you.

When an issue is owner responsibility, we coordinate with our vetted vendor network and handle it. Your tenant's experience in the property reflects on your investment — we treat it that way.

CostsWho pays for repairs — and do you need my approval every time?+

Maintenance costs are the owner's responsibility unless the damage is clearly tenant-caused, in which case we pursue recovery through the deposit or lease remedies.

Approval works in tiers:

  • Routine repairs: Handled and you're notified. We don't need your signature on a broken garbage disposal — waiting creates delays, vendor trip charges, and frustrated tenants.
  • Large-scale work (roof, HVAC replacement, major renovations): We get bids, present options, and proceed only with your authorization.
  • Emergencies: Handled immediately. Florida law requires it — we notify you as soon as possible, but we cannot wait for approval when safety or property integrity is at risk.
CostsHow does the money move when a repair happens?+

Repair costs are drawn from your $500 owner reserve and from rental proceeds as they come in. Most of the time, costs are simply deducted from your monthly rent before disbursement — no action needed from you at all.

If a repair requires funds beyond what's available in the reserve and current proceeds, we'll send you a request and you can pay directly through your owner portal online. This is uncommon, but the portal makes it straightforward when it happens.

Every vendor invoice appears as a clear line item on your monthly owner statement. Nothing is lumped together, nothing is unclear.

The reserve exists so a $120 plumbing call doesn't require a three-day approval process. It's your money, set aside for your property, and it keeps things moving for your tenant and for you.

CostsWhat's a realistic maintenance budget to plan for?+

A useful planning rule: budget roughly 1% of your property's value per year for maintenance. On a $350,000 home, that's $3,500/year — about $290/month set aside mentally.

In reality, most months will see nothing significant. Occasionally a year includes a larger item — HVAC service, water heater replacement, exterior painting. The 1% rule smooths that out over time.

Our maintenance triage process is specifically designed to minimize unnecessary spend — troubleshooting before dispatching, batching small repairs, and using a vetted vendor network with fair, consistent pricing.

InspectionsWhat inspections happen during the tenancy?+

Documentation is non-negotiable at Verandah. Our team's performance is measured on it. Every inspection report belongs to you and is uploaded directly to your owner portal.

  • Move-in inspection: Fully documented with photos before your tenant takes possession. This becomes your property's baseline — your record, stored in your portal and protected.
  • Interim inspections: Conducted while the tenant is in place to confirm the property is being maintained properly and to catch anything that needs attention early.
  • Risk mitigation inspections: Proactive inspections to identify and address any liability concerns before they become problems — fully documented with photos and a written report, uploaded to your portal.
  • Move-out inspection: Handled to the same standard as move-in. The direct comparison gives us the documentation needed to fairly and accurately apply any security deposit deductions beyond normal wear and tear.

All reports are yours. They live in your portal, available to you any time.

You've made it through the hardest part — finding and placing a great tenant. Now we work to keep them. A good, stable tenancy is worth protecting, and that's exactly what we do. When the time does come for a transition, we handle it cleanly so the cycle continues without disruption to you.

We start the renewal process about 80 days before your lease expires — well ahead of the deadline, so there's no scramble. Move-outWhat happens when a tenant leaves?+

When a tenancy ends, we handle the entire process — from coordinating the move-out with your resident to completing the final inspection and closing out their account properly.

The move-out inspection is documented to the same standard as move-in — the direct comparison gives us everything we need to handle any deposit deductions fairly, accurately, and with full documentation to back it up.

The security deposit settlement process is handled in strict compliance with Florida Statute, which has very specific procedures and defined timelines. We follow them carefully — because how this is handled protects you legally and reflects on the integrity of how we manage on your behalf. Once it's closed, the cycle starts fresh, and we carry everything we've learned into the next placement.

Move-outWhat if a tenant needs to leave before their lease is up?+

Our lease does not include a clause allowing tenants to simply break it. They are fully responsible for rent and lease obligations until they are replaced by a well-qualified tenant we've approved.

If a tenant needs to exit early, we manage that process — including finding a replacement and ensuring the financial exposure to you is minimized as much as possible. The tenant remains on the hook until a qualified replacement is in place.
